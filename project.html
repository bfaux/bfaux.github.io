Vine Gives Users A Quick Laugh

With the rise of technology and the dominance of social media in popular culture, fads come and go rapidly. Life goes by at a clip and it becomes difficult to keep with everything that is being thrown at us. In the modern, fast paced world something can suddenly rise in popularity and then vanish just as quickly. There are numerous examples of internet crazes that have exhibited staying power such as YouTube, Facebook, and Twitter but it always difficult to tell how long these things can last. Vine content mostly includes comedy, short sketches, singing, and capturing pranks or events in bite-sized pieces.

Enter Vine, a smartphone app from the makers of Twitter that was started at the beginning of the year and has steadily risen in popularity. Vine allows users to create six-second mini-movies that run on an endless loop. Perhaps the appeal is hard to see at first but "viners" quickly learned that the short six-second format calls for a certain degree of creativity and innovation to land a punchline or make a point. Vine content mostly includes comedy, short sketches, singing, and capturing pranks or events in bite-sized pieces As with YouTube and Twitter Vine has created internet celebrities some that have even reached millions of followers.

Vine has even become useful for journalism. According to mashable.com, "the app got its first foray into breaking news in April when users captured footage of explosions during the Boston Marathon bombings". As with Facebook, Youtube, and Twitter advertisements have begun cropping up throughout Vine as well. According to blog.brandsonvine.com companies like Dunkin Donuts and Trident started using vine to advertise their products.

Because so many people have access to social media, internet memes, apps, and viral videos, something can become a phenomenon quickly and unexpectedly. Vine has created a number of internet stars. Some have millions of followers. One Vine sensation, fifteen year old Nash Grier, was featured on ABC News in October 2013 after amassing 1.4 million followers.
